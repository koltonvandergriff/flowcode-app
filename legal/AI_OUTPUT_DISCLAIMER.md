## 1. AI output can be wrong

FlowADE provides interfaces to AI providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, and any provider you configure with your own API key) and hosts terminal sessions for AI coding tools such as the official Claude Code CLI, Aider, and similar.

AI providers can and do produce output that is:
- **Inaccurate** — factually wrong, hallucinated, or made up.
- **Incomplete** — missing edge cases, error handling, or essential context.
- **Insecure** — code with vulnerabilities, including command injection, SQL injection, XSS, hardcoded secrets, or unsafe permissions.
- **Infringing** — content that may inadvertently reproduce copyrighted material from training data.
- **Biased** — reflecting biases present in training data, prompts, or model architecture.
- **Outdated** — based on training cutoffs that may predate current best practices, APIs, or vulnerabilities.

You must review AI-generated output before you act on it.

## 4. FlowADE's role

FlowADE is a workspace. We host terminals, organize tasks, route prompts through provider APIs using your keys, and store memory entries you create. We do not curate, validate, or stand behind AI provider output. We exclude liability for AI output to the extent permitted by law, as described in the Terms of Service.

## 5. Practical safeguards we include — but don't rely on alone

- **Per-action approval** is the default for shell commands generated by agentic CLIs.
- **Secret-pattern scrubbers** attempt to detect API keys, PATs, and private keys before they're stored in memory or sent to embedding providers.
- **Workspace isolation** keeps terminals scoped to their workspace's working directory by default.
- **Memory categorization** uses your configured AI provider, and the text of your memories travels to that provider for classification.

These are mitigations, not guarantees. They may miss cases.
